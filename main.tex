\begin{abstract}
\thispagestyle{plain}
\noindent Anomalous quartic gauge couplings (aQGCs) provide a model-independent parametrisation of physics beyond the Standard Model in the self-interactions of the electroweak gauge bosons. Vector boson scattering (VBS) is the most sensitive probe of these couplings at the LHC. This thesis extends that probe to the fully hadronic VBS channel, the final state with the largest combined branching fraction but also with the most overwhelming QCD multijet background, using the full ATLAS Run-2 dataset of $140\,\mathrm{fb}^{-1}$ at $\sqrt{s}=13\,\mathrm{TeV}$.

A two-stage classifier chain is developed. A six-jet VBF-RNN tagger combines the kinematics of the forward tagging jets with the two large-$R$ signal jets to identify the VBS topology in an operator family independent manner, raising the inclusive AUC from $0.760$ to $0.801$ relative to a four-jet baseline. A subsequent MLP discriminant separates the aQGC signal from the composite background using twelve event-level input features. Three architectures are evaluated: specialised binary classifiers trained per operator family, a global binary classifier covering all $19$ operators in the Éboli dimension-eight basis, and a four-node multi-class classifier that resolves the scalar, mixed and tensor operator families separately. An equal-signal-cross-section reweighting addresses the operator-level imbalance inside each training category.

The Asimov sensitivity of every discriminant on every probed operator is compared to the $m_{JJ}$-only baseline computed under the same binning prescription and a $30\%$ Gaussian background normalisation uncertainty. The multivariate discriminants improve the expected significance by factors of $11$ to $281$ depending on the operator and architecture. The equal-signal-cross-section reweighting is most consequential for the multi-class architecture, which gains the most of the three classifiers on the tensor family in the final configuration and there reaches $0.79$ to $0.93$ of the specialised model's significance, becoming competitive with the dedicated binary classifiers. The choice between the three architectures is deferred to the analysis proper.
\end{abstract}
