\begin{abstract}
\thispagestyle{empty}
\noindent This thesis investigates machine learning methods to probe anomalous quartic gauge couplings (aQGC) in vector boson scattering processes at the ATLAS experiment. Anomalous quartic gauge couplings provide a model-independent window onto physics beyond the Standard Model, parametrising possible deviations in the self-interactions of electroweak gauge bosons. Vector boson scattering offers a sensitive probe of these couplings, but the relevant signal is rare and sits beneath large Standard Model backgrounds. We develop and evaluate machine learning classifiers to enhance the separation between signal and background, and assess their impact on the sensitivity to the anomalous coupling parameters.
